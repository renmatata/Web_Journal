COMPUTER VISION

DEFINITION:

Computer Vision is a field that focuses on developing algorithms that enables computers to interpret and understand visual information. It involves the use of mathematical models, machine learning, and computer science to perform tasks such as image recognition and object detection.

TECHNIQUES

  • Image Segmentation : the process of dividing an image into multiple segments or regions to facilitate analysis

  • Feature Extraction: the process of identifying distinctive characteristics, or features, within an image to help identify or classify objects

  • Machine Learning: the use of algorithms and statistical models to enable computers to learn from data and improve performance

  • Convolutional Neural Networks: a type of deep learning neural network commonly used in image classification and object detection tasks

APPLICATIONS OF COMPUTER VISION

  • Healthcare:It has the potential to transform the way we diagnose and treat illnesses, leading to better patient outcomes and reduced healthcare costs.

  • Retail: It helps retailers analyze customer behavior, optimize store layouts, and automate supply chain operations.

  • Automotive:It enables vehicles to recognize and respond to their surroundings, detect obstacles, and navigate roads safely.

  • Entertainment: It enables the creation of immersive experiences and enhances the realism of special effects.

Cyber Security

DEFINITION:

Cybersecurity refers to the protection of electronic devices, networks, and sensitive information from unauthorized access, theft, and damage. It involves using technologies, processes, and practices to prevent and mitigate cyber threats. The goal of cybersecurity is to ensure confidentiality, integrity, and availability of digital assets.

CYBER THREATS

  • Malware: Is a malicious software that is designed to disrupt, damage, or gain unauthorized access to a computer system or network. Examples include viruses, Trojans, and ransomware.
  • Phising:Is a type of social engineering attack where an attacker poses as a trustworthy entity to deceive victims into providing sensitive information.
  • Social Engineering: Is the use of psychological manipulation techniques to trick people into divulging confidential information or performing an action that is not in their best interest.
  • Insider Threats: Insider threats are threats that come from within an organization. They can be accidental, such as a mistake made by an employee, or intentional, such as a malicious employee stealing sensitive information.
  • Advanced Persistent Threats: APTs are targeted attacks that are carried out by sophisticated and persistent adversaries. APTs are designed to remain undetected for long periods of time, allowing the attacker to gain access to sensitive information and carry out their objectives.

Additive Manufacturing

DEFINITION:

Also known as 3D printing, is a revolutionary technology that has transformed the manufacturing industry. This technology enables the production of complex geometries that are difficult or impossible to make with traditional manufacturing methods. In this report, we will discuss the different types of additive manufacturing technologies, their advantages and limitations, and their potential impact on various industries.

TYPES ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING

  • Fused Deposition Modeling: This technology involves heating and extruding a thermoplastic material through a nozzle to create the desired shape layer by layer.
  • Stereolithography: This technology uses a liquid photopolymer resin that is cured by a UV laser to create the desired shape layer by layer.
  • Selective Laser Sintering: This technology uses a laser to selectively fuse powdered material, typically nylon or metal, to create the desired shape layer by layer.
  • Electronic Beam Melting Point: This technology uses an electron beam to selectively melt a metal powder, typically titanium, to create the desired shape layer by layer.

APPLICATION OF ADDITIVE MANUFACTURING

  • PROTOTYPING: Allows for the rapid production of complex geometries, multiple design iterations, and improved accuracy and precision, all of which can significantly reduce the time and cost of developing new products.
  • HEALTHCARE:
    • Customized medical implants.
    • Surgical planning using 3D models.
    • Tissue engineering with 3D-printed scaffolds.
  • AUTOMOTIVE: It enables vehicles to recognize and respond to their surroundings, detect obstacles, and navigate roads safely.
  • EDUCATION: It allows students to visualize and create physical objects, improving their understanding of complex concepts and preparing them for future careers in engineering, design, and manufacturing.
